GreenThumb Software Project Management Plan (Version 1.0)

1. Introduction

1.1 Overview

Green Thumb is a mobile application capable of running on Android devices. It allows users to upload a photograph of a plant and have its species determined by image recognition and machine learning, as well as browse a database of plants. Further, registered users can upload their photographs to the appropriate species’ page, where they can be seen by other users.

This project has no budget.

The management sub-team is responsible for overseeing the progress of the project as a whole.

3.2 Monitoring, Control Mechanisms

The backend, frontend, and machine learning teams have weekly meetings of one hour to discuss scheduling, progress, and issues. The management team has weekly meetings no shorter than one hour to review the progress of the project as a whole. The QA sub-team does not have scheduled meetings, but communicates over the messaging platform, Slack.

A shared repository is used to keep all member contributions together as well as document changes to the project and maintain previous versions.

4.2 Project Staff Training

The managers of each team are responsible for ensuring that each of their team members has the necessary skills to contribute to their part of the project. Individuals with relevant skills on each team are in charge of teaching their teammates, either during the group meetings or during separate meetings which fit the schedules of those involved.

4.3 Quality Control

The QA sub-team designs and assigns tests to the other members of the group and records the results of these tests. In the case that a test fails, the creator of the code is notified by the QA team so changes can be made before the code is re-tested.

In the case of non-code portions of the product, the QA team assigns an individual who is not the creator to read over the writing and compare it with the official style guide as well as check for any spelling or grammar mistakes. This person must fix any inconsistencies and log what changes were made to improve the document.
